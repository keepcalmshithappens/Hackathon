What even is vaporwave?

Vaporwave is a microgenre of electronic music, visual art style, and Internet meme that emerged in the early 2010s. It is defined partly by its slowed-down, chopped and screwed samples of smooth jazz, elevator, R&B, and lounge music from the 1980s and 1990s. The surrounding subculture is sometimes associated with an ambiguous or satirical take on consumer capitalism and pop culture, and tends to be characterized by a nostalgic or surrealist engagement with the popular entertainment, technology and advertising of previous decades. Visually, it incorporates early Internet imagery, late 1990s web design, glitch art, anime, 3D-rendered objects, and cyberpunk tropes in its cover artwork and music videos.

The art of vaporwave challenges me to think creatively and push the boundaries of my own creativity, as I explore new techniques and ideas to bring this aesthetic to life.

One of the things I love about vaporwave is its versatility. It can be used to create a wide range of artwork, from digital illustrations to traditional paintings and sculptures. This versatility allows me to explore different mediums and techniques while staying true to my creative vision.

Another aspect of vaporwave that I find inspiring is its ability to evoke emotion and nostalgia. The bright colors, glitchy imagery, and hazy soundscapes of vaporwave transport me to a different time and place, and evoke powerful feelings of nostalgia and longing. As an art student, I am grateful for the inspiration and opportunities that vaporwave has given me, and I look forward to continuing to explore this aesthetic in my own work.
